Bangladesh, officially the People's Republic of Bangladesh,
Location: South Asia, on the Bay of Bengal.
Area: Around 147,630 sq km (similar to England & Wales).
Topography: Mostly flat, fertile alluvial plains, with hills in the southeast.
Population: Over 170 million, making it one of the world's most densely populated countries.
Capital: Dhaka.
Key Industries: Ready-made garments (world's second-largest exporter), textiles, jute, pharmaceuticals, and agriculture.
Economic Growth: Significant progress in reducing poverty and improving health, now a lower-middle-income country.
Microfinance: A pioneer in microcredit, with institutions like Grameen Bank.
Language: Bengali (Bangla) is the official language.
Religion: Majority Muslim, with a growing secular/atheist population facing challenges.
Education: High literacy rates, though gender gaps persist.
History & Governance :
Independence: Gained independence from Pakistan in 1971 after a liberation war.
Government: Parliamentary democracy with a President (Head of State) and Prime Minister (Head of Government).
